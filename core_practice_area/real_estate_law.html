Real Estate Law

At our firm, we understand the challenges faced by landlords, tenants, entrepreneurs, and farmers in today's rapidly changing real estate market. With soaring property prices, rising rent rates, and increasingly complex lease agreements, many renters and property owners are struggling with escalating costs. Specifically, issues related to high annual rent increases have become a pressing concern for both landlords and tenants alike.

Entrepreneurs, small business owners, and farmers often find themselves caught between the pressures of maintaining their property leases and the rising costs that come with them. These increases can significantly impact the viability of their businesses and farming operations, particularly in sectors with limited income margins. We provide legal counsel to help both property owners and tenants navigate these challenges effectively and ensure that their rights are respected and upheld.

For landlords, it is essential to understand local regulations governing rent increases, including rent control laws and market conditions that may limit how much they can raise rent annually. On the other hand, for tenants and small business owners, we provide advice on how to address unfair rent increases, negotiate rent reductions, or explore options to challenge excessive rent hikes in accordance with applicable laws.

Types of Real Estate Transactions

Real estate transactions are often among the most significant investments in a person’s or business’s life. Whether you are buying or selling residential or commercial property, it's essential to have legal professionals who are well-versed in real estate law to ensure your interests are protected. Here are the primary types of real estate transactions we handle:

Residential Real Estate Transactions

Residential real estate transactions are the most common type of property deal, involving the buying and selling of homes, condominiums, and other types of residential properties. These transactions often include negotiations, drafting contracts, reviewing inspection reports, and securing financing. Our team assists clients throughout every phase of a residential property deal, ensuring that all terms are clear, fair, and legally binding.

For individuals looking to purchase or sell their home, we provide guidance on everything from reviewing the purchase agreement to addressing any issues that may arise during the closing process. We also ensure that all disclosures are made in accordance with the law, and we help clients understand their rights and obligations. Our attorneys can also assist with negotiations, ensuring you get the best possible deal while avoiding any legal pitfalls. For more details on home buying or selling, see our Home Buying Guide.

Commercial Real Estate Transactions

Commercial real estate transactions involve properties that are used for business purposes, including office buildings, retail spaces, industrial properties, and more. These transactions are typically more complex than residential deals and often require a higher level of expertise. Our attorneys specialize in negotiating and structuring commercial real estate transactions, ensuring that every detail is addressed in the purchase agreement or lease contract.

When handling commercial real estate deals, we assist clients with various aspects such as financing, property valuation, lease negotiations, zoning issues, and more. We also offer advice on business entity formation (e.g., LLCs or corporations) to ensure the appropriate legal structure is in place before a transaction is finalized. We work with real estate investors, developers, and business owners to ensure that their transactions are legally sound and achieve their financial goals.

Real Estate Disputes

Real estate disputes can arise at any stage of the property ownership or transaction process. Whether it’s a disagreement between a landlord and tenant, a dispute over property boundaries, or a conflict between property buyers and sellers, real estate disputes can be costly and stressful. Our firm is equipped to handle a wide range of real estate disputes, providing aggressive legal representation and working toward effective solutions.

Landlord-Tenant Disputes

One of the most common types of real estate disputes involves conflicts between landlords and tenants. These disputes can arise over issues such as unpaid rent, security deposit disputes, lease violations, eviction proceedings, and more. Our firm helps both landlords and tenants navigate these issues in accordance with local and state laws, ensuring their rights are protected.

Landlord-tenant law is governed by specific rules and regulations that vary by jurisdiction. We are well-versed in these rules and can help our clients understand their legal options. For landlords, we assist with enforcing the terms of the lease, handling evictions when necessary, and ensuring compliance with tenant rights laws. For tenants, we provide legal support in situations such as disputes over habitability, wrongful eviction, or issues with lease terms. For more on this, refer to our detailed Tenant Rights page.

Property Boundary Disputes

Disagreements over property boundaries can create significant legal challenges. Property owners may dispute the location of property lines, easements, or encroachments, leading to legal battles over land ownership. Our attorneys have experience handling boundary disputes, helping clients resolve these issues through negotiation, mediation, or, if necessary, litigation.

Boundary disputes can involve complex legal concepts such as adverse possession, land surveys, and easements. Our team works closely with surveyors and other experts to accurately determine property boundaries and provide legal strategies for resolving disputes. We aim to resolve boundary conflicts in a way that protects your property rights and minimizes the need for costly court proceedings.

Title Disputes

Title issues are another common source of real estate disputes. A clear and marketable title is crucial when buying or selling a property. However, disputes can arise over unclear title ownership, defects in the title, or competing claims to ownership. These disputes can complicate the buying or selling process and potentially derail a deal.

Our real estate attorneys help clients clear up title issues by conducting thorough title searches and working with title companies to resolve any defects or encumbrances. We also provide legal representation in cases where clients are facing competing claims to property ownership. If necessary, we help clients initiate litigation to quiet title and establish clear ownership of their property.
